verify
/ˈvɛr.ɪ.fʌɪ/ · verb
  1. to establish the truth or correctness of something through rigorous, repeatable testing.
  2. (in silicon) to prove a design behaves exactly as intended — across every corner, every cycle, every condition — before it becomes permanent.
01 — About

Precision is the whole job.

I work as a Design Verification Engineer at NXP Semiconductors in Bangalore, verifying mixed-signal automotive ICs , LED controllers and battery-management systems - the kind of silicon that has to be right because it ends up in cars. Day to day that's SVA properties in Cadence JasperGold, coverage closure, protocol testbenches, and Python/AI automation — and owning verification through five successful tapeouts, bring-up to sign-off.

That verification mindset , assume nothing, test the boundaries, trust only what you can prove bleeds into everything else. Before NXP I built audio-EQ and voice-detection ML at Texas Instruments and a tyre-inspection vision system at Tata Motors; on my own time I tinker with classifiers, IoT alerts, and writing about ideas I can't stop chewing on.

I'm a BITS Pilani, Hyderabad alumnus (B.E. Electronics & Instrumentation, minor in Robotics & Automation), happiest when I'm pulling apart a problem that sits just outside whatever I already know how to do.
